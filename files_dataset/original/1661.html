The first practical incandescent light bulb was invented by Thomas Edison in 1879. Edison experimented with different materials, including carbon filaments, until he found one that could sustain a stable and long-lasting electric current. His design used a carbon filament in a vacuum-sealed glass cylinder, which was powered by an electric current from a direct current (DC) battery. Edison's invention quickly gained popularity due to its convenience and safety compared to earlier light sources such as candles and gas lamps. It also marked the beginning of the electrification of homes and businesses, leading to significant improvements in productivity and quality of life. Over time, advancements in light bulb technology led to more efficient and longer-lasting bulbs. Today, there are various types of light bulbs available, including LED, halogen, and fluorescent bulbs, each with their own unique characteristics and benefits. In summary, the invention of the light bulb by Thomas Edison in 1879 revolutionized the way people lit their homes and workplaces, making it safer, more convenient, and more accessible to the masses.
