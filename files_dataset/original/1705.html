This allowed the filament to heat up more evenly and last longer. On October 21, 1879, Edison officially lit up his laboratory in Menlo Park, New Jersey, with the first commercially practical incandescent light bulb. His design quickly became the standard for indoor lighting, replacing gas lamps and other types of light sources.
