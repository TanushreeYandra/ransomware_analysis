These systems were widely adopted in industries such as banking, healthcare, and manufacturing. However, they had limitations, such as being unable to handle uncertainty or ambiguity. 3. Machine Learning (1990s-Present): With the rise of machine learning, AI shifted its focus from explicitly represented knowledge to learning from data. This allowed AI systems to improve their performance over time through trial and error. Popular machine learning techniques include decision trees, neural networks, and deep learning. Today, AI is applied in various areas, including natural language processing, computer vision, robotics, and autonomous vehicles. 4. Current Trends (2010s-Present): Recent advancements in AI have led to the development of narrow or weak AI, designed for specific tasks like image recognition, speech recognition, or playing games. To address these concerns, researchers are working on developing more transparent, explainable, and ethical AI systems. 5. Future Prospects: The future of AI looks promising, with potential applications in fields like medicine, space exploration, and climate modeling.
