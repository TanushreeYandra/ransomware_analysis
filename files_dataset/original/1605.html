He developed a vacuum pump to remove the air from inside the bulb, which greatly increased its efficiency and lifespan. The bulb could burn for over 12 hours using a single piece of carbon, making it a much more practical solution than earlier experiments. Over the next few years, Edison improved upon his design, increasing the number of carbon filaments and experimenting with different materials like cotton thread and bamboo. By 1882, he had developed a light bulb that could burn for over 40 hours using a single filament. Other inventors soon followed suit, improving upon Edison's design and experimenting with different materials. In 1883, a man named Lewis Howard Latimer patented a light bulb that used a tungsten filament, which lasted even longer than Edison's design. The light bulb quickly became an essential part of everyday life, revolutionizing industries like transportation, communication, and entertainment.
