When an electric current was applied to the filament, it glowed brightly due to the heat generated by the flow of electrons. Over the next few years, Edison and his team refined their design, improving the efficiency and lifespan of the light bulb. By 1882, they had developed a commercially viable light bulb that could burn for hours using a relatively low amount of electricity.
