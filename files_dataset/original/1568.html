His team tested numerous materials, including cotton thread, cardboard, and even bamboo, but none were suitable for mass production. On October 21, 1879, Edison finally succeeded in creating a working light bulb using a carbon filament. He patented his design on November 4, 1879, and the rest is history. The light bulb quickly became an essential item in homes, businesses, and public spaces, revolutionizing the way people lived and worked.
